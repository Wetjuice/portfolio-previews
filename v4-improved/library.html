Portfolios worth studying — curated by craft, not credential. Game directors, UX designers, and creative leads who've figured out how to present senior-level work without overselling it.

Game Director & Creative Director
Jenova Chen — thatgamecompany
jenovachen.info · Journey, Flower, Sky. Minimalist layout, elegant typography, narrative-driven presentation. Clean and authoritative — lets the work speak without decoration.
Visit →
Why It's Here

A masterclass in restraint. When the work is this good, the site just needs to get out of the way. Every page choice is invisible, which means every design decision was correct.

Dean Tate — Valve
iamdeantate.com · BioShock, Half-Life: Alyx. Dark theme, parallax scrolling, strong case study structure. Good example of a senior designer leading with project context and detailed role breakdowns.
Visit →
Why It's Here

The case study structure is worth stealing — project context first, then role, then outcomes. Dark theme done right: not stylistic darkness, but darkness that makes the work pop.

David Shaver — Epic Games
davidshaver.net · The Last of Us II, Titanfall, Fortnite. Interactive grid layout, modern and browsable. Clean navigation with project images leading to detailed case studies.
Visit →
Why It's Here

The interactive grid makes breadth of work browsable without being overwhelming. Good reference for how to handle multiple AAA titles without the page becoming a list of logos.

Ephraim Joseph — DEPT® Agency
ephraimjoseph.com · Switchable dark/light theme, interactive text, vertical menu. 20+ years of experience — good reference for senior-level positioning and breadth of work presentation.
Visit →
Why It's Here

How to position breadth without losing identity. The dark/light theme toggle is a subtle signal: "I think about the user experience of my own portfolio." Noted.

Gavin Strange — Aardman
jinkys.co · Neon accents, bold personality-forward aesthetic. Strong creative identity without sacrificing legibility. Shows how aesthetic can itself be a portfolio piece.
Visit →
Why It's Here

Proof that personality-forward doesn't mean unprofessional. The site IS the creative statement. Not right for every director's positioning — but worth understanding why it works here.

Product Manager & Product Designer
Simon Pan
simonpan.com · Case study-driven, typography-led, zero visual noise. Sets the standard for PM/PD portfolios — focuses on problem-solution narratives over flashy visuals.
Visit →
Why It's Here

The gold standard. Every case study is a problem-solution narrative, not a feature showcase. Transferable to any cross-industry UX pitch — this is the format that reads as "senior."

Jordan Jenkins
jordanjenkins.co.uk · Large-scale visuals, generous whitespace, crisp typography. One of the cleanest modern product design sites. Complex workflows presented clearly.
Visit →
Why It's Here

Whitespace as a design choice, not a lack of content. The generous breathing room signals confidence — "the work needs no decoration."

William Bout — Vercel
williambout.me · Vertical scroll feed — project images zoom into focus as you scroll. Slick, frictionless, lets the work reveal itself naturally without forced navigation.
Visit →
Why It's Here

The scroll-to-zoom interaction is clever without being gimmicky — it serves discovery, not decoration. Good lesson: interactions earn their place by improving comprehension.

Bradley Ziffer
bradleyziffer.com · Subtle animations, storytelling-forward. Calls projects "recent adventures" — personality through copy, not just design. Clean layout with bouncy hover states.
Visit →
Why It's Here

"Recent adventures" instead of "projects" — one copy choice that makes the whole site feel warmer. Small decisions that compound into a distinctive voice.
