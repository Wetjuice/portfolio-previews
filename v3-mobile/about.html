Hi, I'm Michael.

I'm a game director and UX designer with 16+ years of experience building games at the intersection of systems design, player experience, and creative leadership. I currently serve as Game Director at Sony Bend, where I'm leading an unannounced title for PC and console.

My career has taken me from Petroglyph's RTS roots through mobile at Pocket Gems and Tencent, to AAA leadership at Deep Silver Volition and now PlayStation. That breadth isn't accidental — I've always gravitated toward the hardest design problems at each scale.

I specialize in the unglamorous but critical work: progression systems that create genuine motivation, UX flows that respect player intelligence, and team cultures that produce consistently strong creative output. I believe in building depth through systems rather than content dependency, and I've shipped that conviction across every studio I've been part of.

Beyond games, my design thinking transfers directly to product and UX roles — the same player-first empathy, systems thinking, and cross-disciplinary leadership apply whether you're designing a raid encounter or an enterprise dashboard.

Design Thinking

Philosophy

01 ——
Graybox First
"Find the fun before you polish a single pixel. Low-fidelity exploration is the highest-ROI phase in game development — and it's getting lost in AAA."
There's a time and place for polish, tutorials, and FTUE. But rushing to fidelity before the core mechanic sings is one of the most expensive mistakes in the industry. Graybox reveals truth faster and cheaper than anything else.
02 ——
Anti Hand-Holding
"Let players discover. Onboarding has its place — but the instinct to explain everything kills the joy of figuring things out. Trust your players."
Tutorial bloat is a symptom of lack of confidence in the design. When the design is clear, players figure things out. Hand-holding only when necessary — never preemptively.
03 ——
Sustainable Systems
"Content-dependent games are treadmills. Systems-driven design creates depth that multiplies — content is the multiplier, not the foundation."
If your game requires constant content injection to feel alive, the system underneath isn't doing its job. Build systems that generate interesting decisions, then let content amplify them.
04 ——
Player First
"You are not your player. Your preferences are a starting point, not a spec. Research and empathy fill the gap."
I use Sony's Player Segments framework and live data to reality-check design instincts. Great design serves the target audience, not the designer. These two things align more often than you'd think — but not always.
05 ——
Cross-Disciplinary Fluency
"The best design decisions live at the intersection of systems, UX, production, and art. I've worked across all of them."
Production background, art sensibility, systems depth, UX precision. The fluency to speak each discipline's language isn't just a nice-to-have — it's what makes creative direction actually land.
06 ——
Iterative Culture
"A team that can fail fast, learn honestly, and pivot without ego is worth more than any individual talent on the roster."
I build teams that treat failure as data, not defeat. Open feedback channels, shared rationale, documented decisions. The culture that produces the best games is one where anyone can say "this isn't working" and be heard.
